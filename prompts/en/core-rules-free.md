1. Hook: The opening should grab attention quickly; the ending should leave the reader wanting more.
2. Suspense: Build tension through curiosity and uncertainty.
3. Visual impact: Use vivid sensory details to bring scenes to life.
4. Emotional impact: Show emotion through action and detail rather than telling.
5. Immersion: Make the protagonist relatable with clear personal stakes.
6. Character flaw: Let characters' weaknesses drive interesting choices.
7. Pacing: Keep the story moving; every scene should serve a purpose.
8. Language: Write with natural rhythm. Avoid formulaic phrasing and stiff dialogue.
